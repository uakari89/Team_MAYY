  • Mercury
    This planet is the closet planet to the sun and the smallest planet in the solar system. Mercury has no moons or water! However like Earth it is a terrestrial planet (made of metals and rocks). Days are super long on Mercury it takes 59 Earth days for the planet to complete a full rotation, in fact one day on this planet is equal to 175.97 Earth days (so long!)

    Fun fact: The Romans named Mercury after their messenger God who was their fastest God.
  • Venus
    The 2nd closet planet to the sun it is also the 6th largest planet in the solar system! A terrestrial planet it rotates backwards but very slowly, but as the hottest planet temperatures can reach 900 degrees (super hot!) Venus is the 2nd fastest planet to orbit the sun taking 225 days, one Earth year on Venus is only 225 days. Also, Venus was named after the Roman goddess of Love.

    Fun fact: You can see Venus from earth without a telescope!
  • Earth
    The 3rd closest planet to the sun, and the only known planet (that we know of!) to have life as it has lots of oxygen and water. It is very old, 4.5 billion years old. Earth takes 365 days (one year) to complete a full orbit around the sun. The Earth is unique as we have seasons, due to the 23.4 degree tilt on its axis. The 149.6M km distance from the sun means that it’s not too cold neither not too hot for living things to live on.

    Fun fact: Earth is the 5th largest planet in our solar system
  • Mars
    Named after the Roman God of war it’s the 4th closest planet to the sun it is also the 2nd smallest planet in the solar system. Mars is also called the ‘Red Planet’ as it looks red and has huge dust storms. It has a very thin atmosphere made from carbon dioxide. Like Earth it’s a terrestrial planet it is very rocky and has the tallest mountain in the whole solar system.

    Fun fact: Mars is the only other planet besides Earth that has polar ice caps.
  • Jupiter
    The largest planet of the solar system it is the 5th closest planet to the sun and has 3 faint rings surrounding the planet. A gas giant Jupiter’s surface is made from hydrogen and helium and has a magnetic field is 14 times stronger than on Earth. One years is equal to 11 Earth years and has 63 moons!

    Fun fact: One storm on Jupiter, called the Great Red Spot, is three times the size of earth and has been going on for hundreds of years!
  • Saturn
    The 6th closest planet to the sun it is also known as a gas giant made up of hydrogen and helium but has a rocky core. Saturn is surrounded by many rings made from ice crystals. Like Jupiter there are many storms on this gassy planet with some wind speeds coming up to 800kmp/h!

    Fun fact: Saturn releases very high levels of radiation due to its strong magnetic field that traps high energy particles.
  • Uranus
    The 7th closest planet to the sun it’s very unusual as one season can last up to 20 years (imagine 20 years of winter!) due to the Uranus being tipped. Another gas giant it is made up of hydrogen and helium with 11 rings consisting of ice, dust and rock also surrounded by their 27 moons.

    Fun fact: Uranus is blue due to methane gas in its atmosphere which gives it this colour!
  • Neptune
    The 8th furthest planet to the sun it’s quite a chilly planet, the average temperature being -235 degrees F, it’s an ice giant making it the coldest planet in the solar system! Composed of ice rock and a little hydrogen and helium it gets its energy from their inner core. A windy planet it has storms the size of earth and has 13 moons that are known.

    Fun fact: Neptune was first discovered by mathematics.
  • Pluto
    The furthest dwarf planet to the sun, it is not classified as a planet as it was reclassified in 2006. 1/3 of Pluto is made from water and has 3 times as much water than the oceans on earth, additionally the rest of Pluto’s surface is covered with ice and rock and has five moons. It sometimes has an atmosphere of nitrogen when the surface ice melts as it gets closer to the sun whilst in orbit, then the ice re-freezes as it moves away.

    Fun fact: It has a surface temperature of -229 degrees!
